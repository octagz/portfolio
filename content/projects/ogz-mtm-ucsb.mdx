# Introduction

In 2025, I was selected as a Fulbright Scholar to pursue the Master of Technology Management (MTM) at the University of California, Santa Barbara. This program is designed for experienced technologists looking to develop the business, leadership, and strategic skills to complement their engineering background.

&nbsp;

# Why the MTM

After 8+ years of engineering experience across three countries — building IoT firmware in Switzerland, leading engineering teams at an Argentine startup, and architecting data platforms at Compass in the US — I recognized that the most impactful technical leaders are those who can bridge engineering execution with business strategy. The MTM program at UCSB provides exactly that bridge.

&nbsp;

# Coursework & Focus Areas

- **Technology Strategy** — frameworks for technology adoption, competitive analysis, and strategic planning
- **Market Validation** — lean startup methodology, customer discovery, and product-market fit
- **Data Science & Decisions** — quantitative decision-making, statistical modeling, and data-driven strategy
- **Communication** — executive communication, pitch development, and stakeholder management
- **Capstone Field Project** — consulting engagement with an external partner organization

&nbsp;

# Building Beyond the Classroom

The MTM program emphasizes applied learning. I'm actively:

- **Founded DaJent** — an AI-powered DJ workflow platform — through the New Venture Competition, applying coursework to real venture creation
- **Networking** with California's tech and innovation ecosystem
- **Developing** the business and leadership toolkit to complement 8+ years of engineering experience
- **Pursuing** the Fulbright mission of cultural exchange and mutual understanding

&nbsp;

# The Fulbright Experience

Being selected as a Fulbright Scholar represents not just an academic opportunity but a responsibility to bridge cultures and perspectives. As an Argentine engineer in the US technology ecosystem, I bring a unique viewpoint on emerging market challenges, resource-constrained innovation, and the global applicability of technology solutions.
