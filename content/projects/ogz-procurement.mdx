# Introduction

The construction industry in Argentina traditionally relied on phone calls, WhatsApp messages, and spreadsheets to manage procurement — a process that was slow, error-prone, and lacked traceability. At Nuqlea, we built a digital procurement platform that brought constructors and manufacturers onto a single system, enabling transparent, trackable transactions at scale.

&nbsp;

# What the Platform Did

### For Constructors
- **Request for Quotes (RFQ)**: Constructors could submit material requirements and receive competitive quotes from multiple manufacturers and distributors simultaneously
- **Order management**: Track orders from placement through delivery with full visibility into status and timelines
- **Spend analytics**: Aggregate view of procurement spending across projects, enabling better budgeting and supplier negotiations

### For Manufacturers & Distributors
- **Lead generation**: Access to qualified demand from active construction projects
- **Quote management**: Streamlined quoting process with templates and pricing tools
- **Order fulfillment tracking**: End-to-end visibility from quote acceptance through delivery

&nbsp;

# Technical Details

The platform was built with React on the frontend and NodeJS with Express on the backend:

- **Real-time notifications**: Websocket-based updates for quote responses, order status changes, and delivery confirmations
- **Multi-tenant architecture**: Supporting constructors, distributors, and manufacturers with role-specific views and workflows
- **Integration with Nuqlea ecosystem**: Connected to the e-commerce platform, product catalog, and AI-powered product matching system

&nbsp;

# Impact

The procurement platform facilitated multi-million dollar transactions in its first year, representing a significant step in digitalizing Argentina's construction supply chain. It was a core part of the broader Nuqlea ecosystem that included the e-commerce site, product matching AI, recommendation system, and data analytics platform.
